Why this matters

A full video-first movie is expensive.

But a full image-only movie can feel too static if not handled well.

So the best GHS middle path is:

use images where motion is not essential

use video where motion matters

use audio design to make both feel connected


That is the real production logic.

Important narration rule

You are also right about this:

The way video is narrated is not the same as the way image is narrated

That should be documented clearly in the movie planner.

Image scene narration

For image-based scenes, narration can do more work.

Because the frame is more static, narration may need to:

describe setting

explain emotion

explain missing movement

guide the viewer into the moment

carry scene progression


Example:

> Tunde stood at the edge of the frozen cliff, the wind pressing hard against his coat as fear and determination fought within him.



That works for an image scene.


---

Video scene narration

For video-based scenes, narration should usually do less.

Because the motion is already visible, narration should be:

lighter

more selective

less descriptive of obvious action

more emotional or transitional


Example: If we already see Tunde jump, narration should not over-explain:

> He jumped. He spread his arms. He fell through the sky.



That is weak because the video already shows it.

Instead narration may do:

> There was no turning back now.



or no narration at all, just:

wind

music

breathing

parachute sound


So yes — image narration and video narration must be treated differently.

How the planner should decide

For each scene, the movie planner should answer:

1. Does this scene need visible motion?


2. Does the viewer need narration to understand it?


3. Can audio carry this scene without expensive video?


4. Is this a premium action scene or a cheaper bridge scene?


5. Should this be image, video, image-to-video, or hybrid?

Example using your Tunde cliff jump idea

Scene 1 — mountain edge

Type: image or image-to-video
Narration: stronger
Why: this is setup, mood, atmosphere
Audio: wind, low suspense, breathing

Scene 2 — close-up before jump

Type: image-to-video or short video
Narration: light or medium
Why: tension is rising, body detail matters
Audio: boot crunch, breath, stronger wind

Scene 3 — jump

Type: video
Narration: minimal or none
Why: action is visible
Audio: air rush, score lift

Scene 4 — freefall

Type: video
Narration: none or very short
Why: motion and sound should dominate
Audio: intense wind, score peak

Scene 5 — parachute open

Type: video
Narration: almost none
Why: the sound and visual payoff are enough
Audio: snap, fabric burst, release

Scene 6 — glide over mountains

Type: video or hybrid
Narration: short emotional line if needed
Audio: softer wind, heroic music

Scene 7 — landing / ending

Type: image-to-video or hybrid
Narration: medium
Why: resolution can be guided again
Audio: landing, calm breath, music release

That is the exact kind of planning GHS should own.
