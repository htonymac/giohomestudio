# PART 1 — MASTER CORE DOCTRINE

## Primary Production Model

**Audio + Image + Video Hybrid Movie**

This is not an optional feature.
This is a **core production format** inside GHS Movie Creator.

---

## Core Production Philosophy

Not every scene should be video.
Not every scene should be still.

The strongest storytelling balance is:

**Images → Setup & Emotion**  
**Video → Action & Movement**  
**Audio → Continuity & Life**

---

## Core Movie Equation

Movie =  
Image Scenes  
+ Motion Scenes  
+ Audio Layers  
+ Narration Strategy  
+ Sound Design  
+ Character Continuity  
+ Scene Intelligence

---

## Core Value of Hybrid Model

This system allows GHS to:

- Reduce video generation cost
- Extend story length without excessive credits
- Maintain cinematic quality
- Improve pacing realism
- Strengthen emotional delivery
- Support scalable production

---

## Core Scene Types (Master Set)

Every scene must be classified as one of the following:

1. Image-Led Scene
2. Video-Led Scene
3. Image-to-Video Scene
4. Audio-Heavy Bridge Scene
5. Hybrid Scene

These are **non-negotiable structural categories**.

---

## Core Narration Doctrine

Narration style **must change by scene type**.

### Image Scene Narration

Narration Role:

- Scene explanation
- Emotional depth
- World-building
- Motion implication
- Environment description

Narration Strength:

**Medium to Strong**

---

### Video Scene Narration

Narration Role:

- Emotional framing
- Minimal support
- Timing emphasis

Narration Strength:

**Minimal or None**

Motion and sound lead the storytelling.

---

### Audio-Heavy Bridge Narration

Narration Role:

- Scene transition
- Suspense support
- Timeline linking

Narration Strength:

**Strong**

Audio leads continuity.

---

## Core Audio Doctrine

Audio is not decoration.

Audio is **structural glue**.

Every scene may include:

- Dialogue
- Narration
- Sound Effects
- Ambience
- Music

Audio continuity must flow across scene boundaries.

---

## Core Cost Rule

Spend motion only where motion creates value.

Use:

- Images for explanation
- Video for movement
- Audio for continuity

Not the opposite.

# Image-First Scene Intelligence

## Typical Uses

- Character introduction
- Flashback
- Environmental setup
- Emotional reflection
- Suspense pause
- Symbolic storytelling

---

## Allowed Image Treatments

- Static still
- Pan movement
- Zoom movement
- Parallax layers
- Light motion overlays
- Caption-driven scene

These reduce cost without weakening narrative power.

---

# Motion-Priority Scene Intelligence

## Typical Uses

- Action sequences
- Physical reaction
- Fight scenes
- Chase scenes
- Dramatic entrances
- Crowd activity

---

## Motion Options

- Full video
- Short burst video
- Image-to-video transformation

Motion is used selectively.

---

# Mixed Image + Video Logic

Some scenes require controlled motion bursts.

Typical structure:

Still → Motion Burst → Still

Used for:

- Impact moments
- Reaction peaks
- Emotional climax transitions

This preserves cost while increasing impact.

---

# Cost-Aware Scene Engine

## Core Question

"Does this scene truly require motion?"

If **No:**

Use:

- Image scene
- Narrated still
- Camera animation

If **Yes:**

Use:

- Short motion clip
- Video sequence
