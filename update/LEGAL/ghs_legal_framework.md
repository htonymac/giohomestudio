# GioHomeStudio / GHS Auto Creator Legal Framework

**Draft Version 1.0**  
**For product, onboarding, approvals, and publishing controls**

---

## Important Note

This is a strong product legal draft for business and operational use. It is **not a substitute for advice from a qualified lawyer** in Nigeria or in any country where the service will be offered. Before launch, this document should be reviewed by counsel for platform, media, privacy, consumer, IP, and AI-related compliance.

---

# 1. Product Identity

**Product Name:** GioHomeStudio / GHS Auto Creator  
**Product Type:** AI-assisted content creation, review, and publishing platform  
**Operator Model:** User-controlled publishing system with mandatory human review before public posting

GioHomeStudio and GHS Auto Creator are software tools that help users create, edit, review, schedule, and publish media content using a combination of user-provided materials, software features, and third-party AI systems.

The platform may process text, images, audio, video, captions, metadata, and platform connection data in order to generate draft content for review.

The platform is designed as an **assisted publishing system**, not as an autonomous publisher.

---

# 2. Core Legal Position

## 2.1 User is the Publisher of Record

For every item of content that is approved and published through the platform, the **user who approves that content is the publisher of record**.

That means the user is responsible for:

- reviewing the final content before publication
- confirming that the content is accurate and appropriate
- ensuring that the content does not violate any law, regulation, or third-party right
- ensuring that the content does not violate the policies of any destination platform
- ensuring that any person shown, imitated, voiced, referenced, or represented in the content is used lawfully and with all necessary permissions

Approval is treated as an intentional editorial act.

## 2.2 No Automatic Public Posting Without Human Approval

The platform must not publicly post content without a clear human approval action tied to the relevant user account.

No draft, generated asset, caption, narration, or transformed image may be treated as publication-ready until the user has:

1. reviewed it,
2. affirmatively approved it, and
3. completed any required confirmation step presented by the platform.

## 2.3 Platform Role

The platform provides tools for generation, editing, delivery, and posting workflow support. It does not independently verify the truth of user claims, ownership of all materials, or legality of every intended publication.

The platform may implement moderation, warnings, policy blocks, or review controls, but those controls do not transfer legal responsibility away from the approving user.

# 3. Eligibility and Account Conditions

A user may not use the platform unless all of the following are true:

- the user is at least 18 years old
- the user has legal capacity to accept these terms
- the user is authorized to use the images, videos, music, likenesses, brand references, and other content submitted
- the user agrees to mandatory human review before publication
- the user agrees not to use the platform for prohibited content or unlawful impersonation

The platform may suspend, restrict, or terminate access where these conditions are not met.

---

# 4. Content Ownership and Permission Rules

## 4.1 User Materials

The user represents and warrants that they have all rights, licenses, permissions, and legal authority necessary to upload, process, transform, publish, or authorize publication of any material they submit to the platform.

This includes, without limitation:

- photographs
- videos
- voice samples
- music or sound files
- logos, brand assets, and product images
- captions and script text
- personal likeness and identity-based media

## 4.2 Third-Party Content

The user must not upload or use material belonging to another person or business unless the user has a valid legal basis to do so.

Examples of restricted use include:

- using another person’s image without consent
- using a celebrity likeness for commercial promotion without authorization
- cloning or imitating another person’s voice without consent
- using copyrighted music, images, logos, or footage without rights or license
- creating fake endorsements or misleading public association

## 4.3 Consent for Real Persons

Where content includes a real person’s face, voice, name, identifiable image, or recognizable likeness, the user must have lawful permission to use that person in the intended manner.

For commercial or promotional use, written consent should be obtained wherever required.

---

# 5. AI-Generated and AI-Modified Content

## 5.1 Nature of AI Output

The platform may use third-party AI systems to generate or modify content. AI systems can produce inaccurate, misleading, offensive, harmful, low-quality, or infringing outputs.

AI output is inherently probabilistic and may contain errors even when prompts appear valid.

## 5.2 No Guarantee of Accuracy or Safety of Output

The platform does not guarantee that generated output will be:

- factually correct
- legally compliant
- non-defamatory
- non-infringing
- safe for publication
- accepted by destination platforms

The user must review all output before approval.

## 5.3 AI Disclosure Responsibility

Where a social platform, regulator, advertiser, partner, or applicable law requires that AI-generated or AI-assisted content be disclosed, the user is responsible for making that disclosure unless the platform expressly performs that disclosure automatically.

---

# 6. Likeness, Voice, and Identity Protection

## 6.1 Strict Consent Rule

The platform must not be used to create, animate, clone, imitate, or commercially exploit the likeness, face, voice, or identity signals of any real person without lawful authorization.

## 6.2 User Likeness Tools

Where the platform offers features such as face-based animation, avatar generation, dance/performance simulation, voice narration using the user’s voice, or image-to-video transformation of a real person, the user must confirm that:

- the person shown is the user, or
- the user has explicit permission from the person shown for that exact use

## 6.3 Prohibited Identity Abuse

The user must not use the platform to:

- impersonate another person
- create fake statements or endorsements
- simulate another person for fraud, defamation, harassment, or deception
- clone a voice without permission
- produce sexualized, exploitative, or humiliating content involving a real person without lawful consent

Any such attempt may result in immediate suspension, deletion of content, and permanent ban.

---

# 7. Mandatory Human Review and Approval Procedure

Before any content is posted, exported for public use, or scheduled for publication, the approving user must:

1. review the final asset in substantially complete form
2. review the caption, overlays, spoken words, and relevant metadata
3. confirm that the content is accurate and appropriate
4. confirm that the user has necessary rights and permissions
5. confirm that publication is lawful and platform-compliant
6. take an explicit approval action in the product

The platform should maintain an approval log recording, where technically feasible:

- account ID
- content ID
- approval timestamp
- terms version accepted
- approval statement version
- selected destinations
- final content version or hash
- device/session metadata

Approval logs are part of the product’s compliance and audit framework.

---

# 8. Prohibited Content and Prohibited Uses

The platform may not be used to create, publish, store, distribute, or facilitate content that:

- is defamatory or knowingly false about a real person or entity
- is fraudulent, deceptive, or misleading in a material way
- infringes copyright, trademark, privacy, publicity, or other rights
- contains non-consensual sexual content
- contains child sexual abuse material or exploitative content involving minors
- sexualizes minors in any way
- promotes violence, terrorism, or criminal conduct
- incites hatred or discrimination against protected groups
- facilitates scams, impersonation, or identity theft
- harasses, blackmails, or threatens another person
- violates any applicable law or regulation
- violates the policies of an integrated social platform

The platform may also prohibit categories of high-risk synthetic media, political manipulation, election deception, or sensitive misinformation depending on jurisdiction, launch strategy, or platform rules.

---

# 9. Safety Moderation and Enforcement Rights

The platform may, in its sole discretion and where legally permitted:

- scan submitted and generated content for policy violations
- block uploads or outputs
- require additional confirmation before generation or posting
- remove or disable access to content
- suspend connected posting features
- freeze or terminate accounts
- preserve logs for compliance, abuse investigation, or legal response

Moderation tools may include automated detection, rule-based controls, manual review, or third-party moderation services.

The platform may act on suspected violations without prior notice where urgent safety, abuse, or legal risk exists.

---

# 10. Social Platform Connections and Tokens

## 10.1 OAuth and Connected Accounts

Where the platform allows connection to Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube, X, Threads, WhatsApp, Telegram, or other services, the user authorizes the platform to perform the actions specifically permitted through those connections.

## 10.2 No Transfer of Platform Liability

Connection to a third-party service does not mean the third-party service endorses the user’s content or assumes responsibility for it.

## 10.3 Token Handling

The platform should use commercially reasonable measures to secure access tokens, refresh tokens, and account connection data. However, no system can guarantee absolute security.

Users must immediately report suspected unauthorized access, compromise, or misuse of their connected accounts.

# 11. Privacy and Data Handling Principles

The platform may collect and process data necessary to operate the service, including:

- account identity data
- phone number or login credentials used for account authentication
- uploaded or selected media
- generated outputs
- approval logs
- prompt history
- moderation results
- connected platform metadata
- usage analytics and troubleshooting logs

The platform should provide a separate Privacy Policy describing categories of data collected, legal basis, retention approach, sharing practices, security measures, and user rights.

Where legally required, the platform should obtain consent before accessing photo libraries, contacts, microphone, camera, notifications, or background media access.

---

# 12. Retention, Deletion, and Investigative Holds

The platform may retain certain records, logs, moderation results, approval records, and connected account metadata for operational, contractual, legal, anti-abuse, or evidentiary purposes.

Even where a user deletes content, the platform may preserve limited records where reasonably necessary to:

- comply with law
- investigate misuse
- defend legal claims
- enforce terms
- maintain fraud and abuse controls

# 13. Intellectual Property Position

## 13.1 Platform Rights

All rights in the platform software, interface, workflow design, branding, documentation, templates, and proprietary systems remain with the platform owner or its licensors.

## 13.2 User Rights in Inputs and Outputs

As between the platform and the user, the user retains rights they already own in their uploaded materials.

Rights in AI-generated outputs may depend on applicable law, provider terms, source materials, and jurisdiction. The platform does not guarantee exclusive ownership, registrability, or enforceability of any generated output.

## 13.3 Provider Terms

Some generated outputs may be subject to third-party provider terms, use restrictions, attribution rules, or commercial use conditions. The user is responsible for understanding and complying with those conditions where applicable.

# 15. Limitation of Liability

To the fullest extent permitted by law, the platform owner, developers, affiliates, contractors, and integrated providers shall not be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, exemplary, special, punitive, or reputation-related damages arising from:

- user-generated or user-approved content
- use or misuse of generated outputs
- publication decisions made by the user
- third-party platform account suspension or rejection
- copyright, privacy, publicity, defamation, or misrepresentation claims arising from user conduct
- business interruption, data loss, or loss of profits

Where liability cannot lawfully be excluded, it shall be limited to the amount paid by the user for the service during the 3 months preceding the event giving rise to the claim, unless a different minimum standard is required by law.

# 16. Indemnity

The user agrees to defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the platform owner, developers, affiliates, employees, contractors, and service providers from and against claims, liabilities, losses, damages, judgments, penalties, costs, and reasonable legal fees arising out of or related to:

- the user’s uploaded materials
- the user’s prompts, instructions, or posting decisions
- the user’s breach of these terms
- unlawful use of another person’s image, voice, or identity
- infringement of intellectual property or publicity rights
- unlawful, defamatory, deceptive, or harmful published content
- misuse of connected social or messaging accounts

---

# 17. Suspension and Termination

The platform may suspend, restrict, or terminate a user’s access immediately where the platform reasonably believes that the user has:

- violated these terms
- created or attempted prohibited content
- misused likeness or voice tools
- engaged in fraud, abuse, or security compromise
- exposed the platform to legal or reputational risk

Termination may include deletion of access, disabling of integrations, freezing of drafts, removal of stored content, and retention of relevant compliance records.

# 18. Product-Specific Approval Statement

Before final publish, the platform should require a clear confirmation substantially in this form:

> I have reviewed this content in full. I confirm that I have the legal right to use all included materials and that the content is accurate, appropriate, and lawful for publication. I understand that I am the publisher of record for this post.

This approval should be logged per content item.

The onboarding flow should include mandatory affirmations substantially in the following form:

### Age Confirmation
I confirm that I am at least 18 years old.

### Rights and Permission Confirmation
I confirm that I own or lawfully control the media, likenesses, voices, and materials I submit, or I have all required permissions to use them.

### Human Review Confirmation
I understand that all public posting must be reviewed and approved by me before publication.

### Publisher Responsibility Confirmation
I understand that once I approve content for publication, I am the publisher of record and accept responsibility for that publication.

### Prohibited Content Confirmation
I will not use the platform for unlawful, deceptive, exploitative, sexually explicit, infringing, defamatory, or non-consensual content.

### AI Use Disclosure Confirmation
I understand that this platform uses AI systems and that I may need to disclose AI-assisted content where required.

# 21. Jurisdiction and Governing Law

This draft should be localized by counsel.

Until localized, the intended default commercial position is:

- governing law: Federal Republic of Nigeria
- dispute venue: courts with competent jurisdiction in Nigeria, unless a valid written business agreement provides otherwise
- injunctive relief may be sought where misuse of IP, identity, or confidential systems occurs

If the platform expands internationally, separate terms or region-specific addenda may be required.

---

# 22. Final Practical Recommendation

For stronger real protection, the product should not rely only on legal wording. It should enforce the rules in software through:

- versioned terms acceptance
- server-side approval logs
- moderation before generation and before posting
- destination-specific posting controls
- audit records
- restricted use of likeness and voice tools
- explicit consent flows
- token security and access review

Good legal text helps. Enforced workflow helps more.
