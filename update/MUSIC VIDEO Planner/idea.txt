For music video, GHS should not do:

**song in → random visuals out**

**song in → music intelligence analysis → visual planning → dance planning → scene planning → review → generation**

That missing part is the **Music Video Intelligence Layer**.

Without it:

* dance style will feel disconnected
* beat sync will feel weak
* chorus moments will be missed
* scene changes will feel random
* motion intensity will be wrong
* captions may come at the wrong time
* visual energy may not match the music
* AI will not know when to push harder or calm down

## Better architecture

The correct flow should be:

### Step 1 — Intake

User provides:

* generated song
* imported song
* beat
* lyrics
* voice + music
* image + music

### Step 2 — Music Intelligence Analysis

GHS extracts:

* BPM
* beat strength
* rhythm profile
* mood
* structure
* energy curve
* danceability score
* visual style score
* commercial vs artistic score

### Step 3 — Recommendation Layer

GHS suggests:

* best video mode
* best dance type
* best pacing
* best caption mode
* best scene count
* best visual energy

### Step 4 — Storyboard / Motionboard

Instead of only storyboard, music video should also have a **motionboard**.

That means:

* where motion gets stronger
* where dance enters
* where camera moves
* where cuts happen
* where chorus visuals explode
* where bridge calms down

### Step 5 — Preview

User sees:

* song map
* dance suggestions
* visual style suggestion
* chorus highlight points
* preview frames/clips
* estimated credits

### Step 6 — Approve

Then generation starts.
