GHS Children Story Video and Hybrid Planner

Clean Product Canvas for Claude Code

Purpose

This document defines how Children Story should work inside GHS.

Children Story already exists conceptually, but it now needs to be clarified into a strictly supervised product mode with a clear structure, child-safe planning logic, rhythm-based narration/text sync, and proper background music support.

This is not a loose creative mode. This is a high-control, child-safe, education-sensitive mode.


---

1. Core Product Structure

When the user clicks Children Story, the system should not open one loose mixed generator.

It should open two controlled branches:

Children Story Video

Children Story Hybrid


These two branches should sit under Children Story.

Meaning of Each Branch

Children Story Video

This is for:

active animated children content

mini children movie content

learning videos

nursery content with motion

phonics and ABC videos with stronger movement

more dynamic scene-based output


Children Story Hybrid

This is for:

storybook-style content

image + narration + text overlay

light-motion children content

read-aloud books

poem and rhyme content

3-letter word learning content

calm educational hybrid output



---

2. Main Product Principle

Children Story mode must be strictly supervised.

This is non-negotiable.

AI must not be allowed to behave loosely here because children content has a higher safety, clarity, and educational responsibility than ordinary creative content.

Required Rule

It must not simply reuse the general generator with light prompt edits.

5. Child-Safe Planner

Purpose

The Child-Safe Planner is the intelligence layer responsible for making sure children content is appropriate, clear, educational, and well-structured.

It should detect and control:

age-appropriateness

reading level

narration tone

visual simplicity

safe color and motion usage

word difficulty

sentence complexity

whether the content is better as Video or Hybrid

whether the content should be soft or active

whether music should be minimal or rhythmic

whether text should be word-level or sentence-level highlighted


It must block or restrict:

sexual or suggestive output

disturbing visuals

horror-like mistakes

unsafe body styling

adult-looking characters for child content

dark aggressive music

sensual narration

confusing fast overlays

non-child-safe props or backgrounds



---

6. Non-Negotiable Rhythm and Sync Rule

This is a hard requirement.

The system must not treat text, narration, and background music as separate loose layers.

They must work in rhythm together.

Required Sync Behavior

overlay text must align with narration timing

word highlight must follow spoken word timing

sentence highlight must follow spoken sentence timing

line-by-line reading must feel coordinated

pause timing must feel intentional

image or motion changes must not break reading rhythm

if background music exists, narration must still stay clear


Critical Rule

Aligned text must rhythm with narration.

Not approximately. Not loosely. Not as an afterthought.

This must be part of the generation and rendering pipeline.

Why this matters

If rhythm is wrong:

children become confused

reading support becomes weak

educational value drops

the content feels messy

the narration and text no longer reinforce each other


This rule is especially important for:

ABC learning

phonics

3-letter words

word families

poems and rhymes

storybook read-aloud

hybrid educational stories

7. Background Music Logic

The previous planning gap must be fixed here.

Background music must be formally handled in Children Story mode.

User Music Choices

The user should be able to choose:

No music

One background music track

Multiple background music tracks where supported


Music Rules

If music is used:

narration remains the priority

music must not overpower the voice

music must support the educational or storytelling rhythm

music must remain child-friendly

music must not feel sensual, dark, aggressive, or club-like

music changes must not disrupt the text/narration sync


Music Types for Children Mode

GHS should provide child-safe music categories such as:

Soft Storybook Bed

ABC Learning Bed

Counting Rhythm

Nursery Rhyme Loop

Gentle Poem Bed

Bright Classroom Bed

Playful Learning Loop

Calm Bedtime Bed


Multiple Music Track Support

If the user selects more than one background music track:

the system must manage transitions cleanly

tracks must not fight each other

narration clarity must remain protected

the rhythm engine must keep narration and text timing stable

music layering must remain simple and controlled for child output


Music Mix Rule

Children mode should use a Narration-First Mix.

That means:

narration is always the main layer

music is supportive, not dominant

educational clarity is more important than musical excitement

15. First Review and Second Review

Children mode must use two review checkpoints.

First Review

This should happen before preview generation goes too far.

It checks:

text interpretation

narration style choice

age fit

visual plan

word difficulty

music suitability

accent suitability

safety of prompt understanding


Second Review

This should happen after preview generation.

It checks:

final visuals

final characters

movement style

background objects

text overlay quality

highlight sync quality

narration quality

background music fit

any unsafe or strange AI mistakes


Reason

Children content needs higher protection because even if text seems safe, visuals or audio may still come out wrong.

16. Accent and Pronunciation Control

Accent and pronunciation must be appropriate for children learning.

Required Support

child-friendly pronunciation

clear phonics-friendly reading where relevant

stable accent selection

educational pronunciation style


Accent Rule

Accent must help the learning experience, not confuse it.

For learning content such as ABC, phonics, 3-letter words, and read-aloud mode, pronunciation clarity is more important than stylish voice design.

22. Required System Engines

To make this work properly, GHS needs these engines.

1. Child-Safe Planner

Decides mode, restrictions, safety rules, and learning suitability.

2. Text Understanding Engine

Understands words, story level, rhyme structure, and educational type.

3. Narration Timing Engine

Maps spoken timing for words, lines, and pauses.

4. Highlight Sync Engine

Aligns overlay text with narration rhythm.

5. Children Music Engine

Selects and controls child-safe background music behavior.

6. Visual Planning Engine

Chooses image/scene style and visual pacing.

7. Review Engine

Runs first review and second review.

8. Export Identity Engine

Applies user name, verification mark, ABC mark, and versioning support.
